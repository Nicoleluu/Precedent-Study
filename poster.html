Final visual synthesis
Data Is Never Neutral
A 36 × 36-inch poster translating the precedent study into one visual argument.
Design Argument

The poster extends the precedent study’s discussion of algorithmic fairness by moving one step earlier in the process. Before a model evaluates fairness, a dataset has already been selected, described, labeled, translated, and limited by human decisions.

01 / Red and blue

No neutral middle

The opposing colors create tension rather than balance. They reject the idea that data exists as a colorless or objective material before interpretation.

02 / Multilingual fields

Values travel through language

Words related to identity, ownership, authority, standards, policy, and governance appear across languages. The texture suggests that data practices are shaped by cultural and institutional vocabularies.

03 / Overlapping forms

Systems select and filter

Large circles, curves, masks, and cropped fields overlap the text. They operate like systems of collection and classification: some information is emphasized, some is transformed, and some disappears.

04 / Missing information

Absence also has meaning

The statement “Missing data is also information” asks viewers to consider who is absent, what was never measured, and which contexts were excluded before analysis began.

Central claim

Before a model learns, someone has already selected, labeled, and excluded.
